{% trans %}Welcome to OpeningParliament.org, a forum intended to help connect the world's civic organizations engaged in monitoring, supporting and opening up their countries' parliaments and legislative institutions.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Parliamentary monitoring organizations, or “PMOs,” are working to create strong, open and accountable parliaments, through enhancing citizen participation in the legislative process and bringing parliaments closer to the people they represent. OpeningParliament.org provides a forum for international collaboration on efforts to improve access to parliamentary information and share experiences and good practices among PMOs. It also serves as the home of the Declaration on Parliamentary Openness (link to ‘Declaration’ tab), a set of shared principles on the openness and transparency of parliaments being developed by the PMO community.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}OpeningParliament.org and the Declaration on Parliamentary Openness are outcomes of a recent conference of PMOs (link to ‘Events’ tab) co-hosted by the National Democratic Institute, the Sunlight Foundation, and Latin American Network for Legislative Transparency, in Washington, D.C. The conference, held from April 30-May 2, 2012, brought together more than 40 PMOs from 38 countries worldwide to facilitate discussion of international strategies for improving access to useable parliamentary information and sharing good practices in advocating for parliamentary transparency and in monitoring parliamentary performance. The gathering was supported by the Omidyar Network, the Open Society Foundation, the National Endowment for Democracy and the Embassy of Mexico to the United States. The World Bank Institute also supported the conference and co-hosted a roundtable at its conclusion. For more information, please see the joint press release issued at the conclusion of the conference.{% endtrans %}
